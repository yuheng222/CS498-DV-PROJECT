Steep rise in housing prices


Another major factor to the declining fertility rates in Singapore is the steep rise in house prices over the last few decades. Singapore is a small city state where land is scarce, so the cost of owning a home has climbed substantially as the population grows. The Price Index for private houses has increased by almost four-fold from 41.25 in 1990 to 152.1 in 2020. More than 80% of Singaporeans stay in public housing, and the Price Index for public houses has increased at a even greater pace; more than 5-fold increase from 24.6 in 1990 to 133.85 in 2020. Since Singapore's average household income only increased about 3-fold from 1990 to 2020, houses have become much less affordable. There is no doubt that the rising housing costs has caused many Singaporean couples to think twice about having children since they might not be able to afford a larger home to accommodate them.
