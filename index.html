Singapore's Baby Shortage


Ever since the 1960s, Singapore's fertility rate has been on a declining trend. Low fertility rate is a problem faced by many countries around the world, especially first world countries. It is a huge concern since a total fertility rate (TFR) of 2.1 is required for each generation to replace itself. If fertility rate falls below this level, then the country is at risk of having an aging population. So what are some of the factors contributing to Singapore's declining fertility rate? And how does Singapore's fertility rate compared to that of other countries?
