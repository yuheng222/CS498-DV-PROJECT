Lifestyle choice of staying single


The main cause of declining fertility rates in Singapore is that more people are choosing to be single. There is a general trend of increasing singlehood across all age groups over the years. There is a inverse relationship between singlehood rates and fertility rates, so declining fertility rates are a direct result of increasing singlehood rates. The 25-29 Years age group has the greatest singlehood rates increase over the years - almost 50% increase from 51.8% in 1990 to 75% in 2020. The most ideal child bearing years of a woman also belongs to this age group, which explains why fertility rates are on a constant downward trend from 1990 to 2020.
