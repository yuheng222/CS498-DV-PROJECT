Fertility Rate Hits An All Time Low


The connected scatterplot below shows Singapore's fertility rate from 1960 to 2020. There is an obvious downward trend over the years. In 1972, the Singapore government started a Stop-at-Two campaign to encourage a two-child family norm by legalizing abortion, imposing significant disincentives for families who chose to have more than two children, and offered rewards to Singaporeans who underwent voluntary sterilization. It was a very effective campaign as fertility rates fell steeply right after. In 1986, the Singapore Government recognised that falling fertility rates were a serious problem, and started the Have-Three-or-More campaign. It had some success in boosting fertility rates for the following two years, but the downward trend continued right after that. Since then, Singapore's fertility rate has not yet been restored to replacement level of 2.1, and it has reached an all time low of 1.1 in 2020. There were some significant upticks in fertility rates for "Years of the Dragon" (1988, 2000 and 2012), which are auspicious years to conceive a baby in the Chinese culture.
